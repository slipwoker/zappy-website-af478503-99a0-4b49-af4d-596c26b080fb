How a loaf becomes a loaf

Every sourdough bread company follows a rhythm—feed, fold, shape, bake. Here's the gentle dance that turns flour and water into the perfect sourdough loaf, step by patient step.

1

Feed starter

The heart of every sourdough bakery: a bubbly, active starter. We feed ours twice daily with equal parts flour and filtered water, watching it double in size and develop that tangy, yeasty aroma that tells us it's ready to leaven.

2

Mix & rest

Combine active starter with bread flour, whole wheat, water, and salt. A gentle hand-mix brings everything together, then we let it rest—autolyse—for 30 minutes so the flour hydrates and gluten begins to form naturally.

3

Stretch & folds

Over the next few hours, we perform a series of gentle stretches and folds—coaxing strength into the dough without kneading. Each fold builds structure, traps air, and develops that signature open crumb we love in artisan sourdough bread.

4

Shape & proof

Once the dough has doubled, we shape it into tight rounds or batards, creating surface tension that helps the loaf rise tall. Then into the fridge for a slow, cold proof overnight—this is where the deep, complex flavors develop.

5

Bake & cool

Straight from the fridge into a blazing-hot Dutch oven. We score the top—a signature pattern—and watch steam work its magic, creating that crackling crust. After 45 minutes, out comes a golden loaf. Now the hardest part: waiting for it to cool completely before slicing.

A few quick answers

Common questions about our sourdough bread and baking process

What makes sourdough bread special?

Sourdough is naturally leavened using wild yeast and beneficial bacteria, creating complex flavors and better digestibility. The long fermentation breaks down gluten and phytic acid, making nutrients more available. It's an ancient method that creates bread with character, depth, and a beautiful crust.

How tangy is your sourdough?

Our sourdough has a mild, balanced tang—not overly sour. The flavor is complex with subtle notes of wheat and a hint of sweetness. We control fermentation time and temperature to create bread that's flavorful without being sharp, perfect for sandwiches or toast with butter.

How should I store my sourdough bread?

Keep your loaf cut-side down on a cutting board or in a paper bag at room temperature for 2-3 days. Avoid plastic bags—they trap moisture and soften the crust. For longer storage, slice and freeze in an airtight container for up to 3 months. Thaw slices as needed.

What's the best way to reheat sourdough?

For crusty loaves, sprinkle the crust lightly with water and warm in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes. For slices, toast them fresh or warm gently in a dry skillet. The crust will crisp up beautifully, and the interior will soften again—almost like fresh-baked.

Any allergen or flour notes I should know?

Our sourdough is made with organic wheat flour and contains gluten. We bake in a shared kitchen, so trace amounts of nuts or seeds may be present. All our breads are dairy-free and egg-free unless noted (like our chocolate sourdough). Check ingredient labels for specific varieties.
