This Week's Journal Entries

Each bake is documented with hydration, fermentation notes, and flavor goals. Every loaf tells a story of time, temperature, and texture—crafted in small batches with care.

Bake #01

Country loaf at 78% hydration, cold-fermented for 18 hours. The crumb opens up beautifully with a creamy interior and a deep caramel crust. Baked Wednesday morning.

Flour blend: 80% bread flour, 20% whole wheat. Aiming for an open, irregular crumb with nutty undertones. Perfect for morning toast with butter and honey.

Serve sliced thick with salted butter, or use for avocado toast. Pairs wonderfully with sharp cheddar and fig jam.

Baked fresh in small batches

Bake #02

Olive and rosemary loaf at 75% hydration, bulk-fermented overnight. Kalamata olives and fresh rosemary folded in during the final stretch. Baked Thursday afternoon.

Flour: 100% bread flour for structure. The olives add moisture and a savory punch, while rosemary brings an earthy, aromatic note. Crust is golden and slightly chewy.

Slice thin for appetizers, or serve alongside pasta and soup. Excellent with olive oil for dipping, or toasted with goat cheese.

Baked fresh in small batches

Bake #03

Seasonal cranberry-walnut loaf at 76% hydration, fermented for 16 hours. Tart cranberries and toasted walnuts create a sweet-savory balance. Baked Friday morning.

Flour blend: 85% bread flour, 15% rye for depth. The cranberries burst with flavor, and the walnuts add crunch. A hint of honey in the dough rounds out the tartness.

Perfect for holiday gatherings or breakfast spreads. Serve with cream cheese and orange marmalade, or toast and top with brie.
