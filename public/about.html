A quiet starter, a louder community

It all began with a simple jar of flour and water on my kitchen counter—a sourdough starter that refused to quit. What started as a curiosity during slow weekends turned into a daily ritual: feeding, folding, watching bubbles rise. I fell in love with the patience fermentation demands and the magic it creates.

Every loaf from Baked by Dev is a small-batch sourdough bread, crafted with time-honored techniques and local organic grains. "Small-batch" means I bake in limited quantities, ensuring each loaf gets the attention it deserves—from the overnight proof to the final golden crust. It's bread that tells a story, one slice at a time.

This isn't just about selling loaves; it's about sharing a craft that brings people together. Whether you're new to sourdough or a longtime fan, I'd love to hear from you. Say hello and let's talk bread.

How it grew

Every loaf is the result of countless hours of practice, patience, and learning from each bake.

Spring 2021 – The starter

It began with a simple jar of flour and water on the counter. Days of feeding, watching bubbles form, learning the rhythm of fermentation. The starter became a living companion, requiring attention and care.

Summer 2021 – First consistent loaf

After dozens of flat, dense attempts, the first real success: a loaf with an open crumb, a crackling crust, and that unmistakable sourdough tang. The kitchen filled with the smell of achievement and possibility.

Fall 2021 – Sharing with friends

Word spread through casual conversations and shared meals. Friends began asking for loaves, offering feedback, requesting different styles. Each request was a vote of confidence and a reason to keep refining the craft.

2022–Present – Weekly bakes & community

What started as a personal challenge became a weekly ritual and a small business. Every Friday, the oven is loaded with loaves for neighbors, friends, and new customers. The sourdough bakery tradition continues, one batch at a time, building community through bread.

What stays the same

In a world of shortcuts and speed, we hold firm to a few simple principles. These aren't marketing points—they're the foundation of every loaf we bake.

Slow fermentation

Every loaf ferments for 24-48 hours. This isn't just tradition—it's how we develop deep flavor, improve digestibility, and create that signature tang.

The difference? A rushed loaf tastes flat. Ours tastes like bread should: complex, slightly sour, with notes that linger.

Ingredient simplicity

Flour, water, salt, starter. That's it. No dough conditioners, no preservatives, no shortcuts. We source organic flour locally and mill some grains ourselves.

When you read our ingredient list, you'll recognize every word. Because if we wouldn't eat it, we won't bake with it.

Community-first pickup

We bake in small batches and coordinate pickup times directly with you. No storefront, no middlemen—just honest communication and bread baked fresh for your schedule.

This means we can answer your questions, accommodate dietary needs, and ensure every loaf is still warm when you pick it up.
